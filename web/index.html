AnyWare: Stacking and Unstacking with SAM3 and UR7

EECS 106A Final Project Team
1University of California, Berkeley
Proposal PDF Video Code

AnyWare utilizes SAM3 to perceive and manipulate objects like boxes and Jenga blocks with a UR7 robotic arm.

Abstract

We present AnyWare, a robotic system capable of autonomously stacking and unstacking boxes and performing delicate manipulation tasks such as unstacking Jenga blocks.

Our approach leverages the state-of-the-art Segment Anything Model 3 (SAM3) for robust object detection and segmentation in unstructured environments. By integrating these vision capabilities with a UR7 Robotic Arm, we enable precise pick-and-place operations.

The system is designed to handle varying object configurations and lighting conditions, demonstrating the versatility of foundation models in robotic manipulation. We aim to push the boundaries of what is possible with off-the-shelf robotic arms and advanced vision models, moving from simple box stacking to the complexity of Jenga game mechanics.

Methodology

Vision System (SAM3)

We utilize SAM3 to generate high-quality segmentation masks of the objects in the workspace. This allows the system to identify individual boxes or blocks, determine their pose (in conjunction with depth data or pose estimation algorithms), and plan grasp points.

Robotic Control (UR7)

The UR7 robotic arm is controlled to execute the pick-and-place trajectories. We employ inverse kinematics to map the target grasp coordinates to joint angles. The control loop integrates visual feedback to adjust for errors and ensure stable stacking.
